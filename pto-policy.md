# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

**Document:** PTO Policy
**Version:** 3.2
**Last updated:** March 4, 2026
**Owner:** People Operations
**Applies to:** All full-time salaried employees, globally
**Contact:** hr@keystone.studio

## 1. Overview

Keystone Studios provides paid time off so you can rest, recharge, handle personal matters, and take care of your health and family. This policy explains how PTO is earned, how it accrues, how to request it, and how Lot Days work alongside it.

Time off at Keystone comes in two forms:

- **PTO** — your standard paid time off, accrued throughout the year
- **Lot Days** — five paid days reserved for the annual lot shutdown between mid-December and early January

Together, these make up Keystone's **"25 + 5"** structure.

## 2. PTO accrual

All full-time salaried employees accrue **25 days (200 hours) of PTO per year**. Accrual is biweekly: you earn approximately **7.69 hours per pay period** (200 hours ÷ 26 pay periods). Accrual begins on your first day of employment — there is no waiting period.

PTO can be used in increments as small as one hour, subject to manager approval.

## 3. The 240-hour accrual cap

PTO accrual caps at **240 hours (30 days)**. Once your balance reaches 240 hours, **accrual pauses** until you use PTO and bring your balance below the cap. Accrual resumes automatically once you drop below 240 hours.

The cap exists for two reasons: to encourage employees to take the time off they've earned, and to limit Keystone's accrued PTO liability. **Unused PTO above the cap is not paid out, banked, or restored** — accrual simply does not happen during the time you are at the cap.

If you are approaching the cap, your manager and People Operations will reach out to help you schedule time off.

## 4. Lot Days

In addition to PTO, every full-time employee receives **5 Lot Days** each calendar year. Lot Days are paid days off tied to the annual lot shutdown — the period each year when the Burbank lot goes dark, typically from a few business days before Christmas through the first business day of January. Exact dates are announced each fall.

Lot Days rules:

- Lot Days **must be used during the lot shutdown window**. They cannot be used at any other time of year.
- Lot Days **do not roll over**. Unused Lot Days expire at the end of the shutdown window.
- Lot Days **cannot be cashed out** under any circumstance, including termination.
- Lot Days are **separate from PTO** and do not draw down your PTO balance.
- Employees in active production who are required to work during the shutdown will receive **equivalent paid time off within 60 days** of the shutdown ending, coordinated through their production manager and People Operations.

Lot Days exist because Keystone closes the lot for the holidays — they are not bonus vacation days, and they are not interchangeable with PTO.

## 5. Holidays (separate from PTO and Lot Days)

Keystone observes **11 paid company holidays** per year, announced annually. Company holidays are paid time off and do not draw down your PTO or Lot Day balance. The full holiday calendar is published each January on the People Operations Slack channel and in Workday.

## 6. Requesting PTO

PTO is requested through Workday and requires manager approval.

- **Requests of 3 days or fewer:** submit at least **5 business days in advance** when possible
- **Requests of more than 3 days:** submit at least **3 weeks in advance** when possible
- **Same-day or short-notice requests** (illness, emergencies, bereavement): notify your manager directly via Slack or phone, then log the time in Workday when you return

Managers are expected to respond to PTO requests within **3 business days**. If you do not hear back, escalate to your skip-level manager or hr@keystone.studio.

## 7. Production blackout periods

Employees assigned to a production in active shoot may have **PTO blackout periods** during principal photography or critical post milestones. Blackouts are set by the production's UPM or showrunner and communicated in writing at the start of the project.

During a blackout:
- PTO continues to accrue normally
- Pre-approved PTO scheduled before the blackout was announced will be honored
- Emergency and bereavement time off is always permitted

## 8. PTO at separation

If you leave Keystone (voluntarily or involuntarily), **unused accrued PTO is paid out in your final paycheck** at your regular base hourly rate, in accordance with applicable state and local law. **Lot Days are not paid out.**

## 9. Frequently confused points

A few items People Operations gets asked about often:

- **PTO does not roll over indefinitely.** It accrues until you hit the 240-hour cap, then pauses. There is no separate "rollover" mechanism on top of accrual.
- **Lot Days are not bonus vacation.** They are tied to a specific window and expire if unused.
- **The 240-hour cap is a hard cap**, not a guideline or a soft target. Accrual genuinely stops at 240 hours.
- **You cannot trade Lot Days for PTO**, or vice versa.
