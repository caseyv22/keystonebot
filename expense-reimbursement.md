# Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Document:** Expense Reimbursement Policy
**Version:** 5.0
**Last updated:** February 10, 2026
**Owner:** Finance — Accounts Payable, in partnership with People Operations
**Applies to:** All full-time salaried employees, globally (production-specific expense rules are governed separately by the UPM on each production)
**Contact:** expenses@keystone.studio | ap@keystone.studio

---

## 1. Overview

Keystone Studios reimburses employees for reasonable, necessary business expenses incurred in the course of their work. This policy defines what is reimbursable, the receipt requirements, the submission process, and the deadlines.

The guiding principle: **spend Keystone's money the way you would spend your own if you were running the business.** When in doubt, ask your manager before incurring the expense, not after.

All expenses are submitted through **Concur**, accessible from Workday SSO.

## 2. What is reimbursable

The following categories are reimbursable when incurred for business purposes:

- **Business travel** — airfare, lodging, ground transportation, baggage fees, business-class upgrades on flights over 6 hours (with manager approval)
- **Meals during business travel** — your meals while traveling for work, subject to per-diem caps (Section 4)
- **Client and business meals** — meals with clients, partners, or candidates, when business is discussed
- **Mileage** — personal vehicle use for business purposes, reimbursed at the current IRS standard mileage rate
- **Conference and professional development** — registration, materials, travel (also see L&D stipend in Perks & Programs)
- **Production supplies and on-set expenses** — governed by your production's UPM, with reimbursement following this policy's process
- **Tools and software** — work-related software, hardware, or subscriptions, with manager pre-approval for items over $200
- **Anniversary Benefit travel stipend** — travel expenses against your $2,500 stipend (see Section 8)
- **Lactation shipping** — milk shipping during business travel for postpartum employees, code `LACTATION-SHIP` (no receipt threshold applies)

## 3. What is NOT reimbursable

The following are **not** reimbursable under any circumstances:

- Personal meals when not traveling for business
- Commuting between your home and your regular work location
- Traffic violations, parking tickets, towing
- Personal entertainment (movies, sporting events, spa services) unless directly tied to a documented client engagement
- Alcohol in jurisdictions where it cannot be expensed under local law, or in any volume that would not be reasonable to a third-party observer
- Personal travel companions' expenses (your partner's airfare, your child's hotel room)
- First-class flights without VP-level pre-approval
- Cash advances or personal loans against future expense reports

If an item is not clearly listed in Section 2 and is not listed here, ask your manager or expenses@keystone.studio before incurring the expense.

## 4. Per-diem and travel caps

Keystone uses **soft per-diem caps** for travel meals — meaning these are the expected maximums, but reasonable variation with documented business reason is acceptable. Per-diem caps cover the full day's meals and incidentals.

| Location tier | Daily per-diem cap |
|---|---|
| US standard cities | $75 |
| US high-cost cities (NYC, SF, LA, Seattle, Boston, DC, Honolulu) | $110 |
| International standard | $90 |
| International high-cost (London, Tokyo, Zurich, Singapore, Sydney) | $130 |

**Lodging** is not subject to per-diem. Book through Keystone's preferred travel agency (Egencia) when possible. Lodging over $400/night requires manager approval before booking.

**Ground transportation** (rideshare, taxi, rental car) is reimbursable at actual cost. Rental car class should be standard / mid-size unless business need justifies otherwise.

## 5. Receipt requirements

**This is the section employees ask about most.** Read carefully.

### The $25 itemized-receipt threshold

- **Any single expense of $25 or more** requires an **itemized receipt** uploaded to Concur. "Itemized" means the receipt shows what was purchased, not just a total charged.
- **Expenses under $25** do not require an itemized receipt, but you must still submit the expense with: date, vendor, amount, and business purpose. A credit card statement line is acceptable in place of a receipt for under-$25 items.

### What "itemized" means

A valid itemized receipt shows:
- Vendor name
- Date
- Line items (what was purchased)
- Subtotal, tax, and total
- Method of payment (last 4 of card is sufficient)

A credit card slip showing only a total amount is **not** an itemized receipt.

### Exceptions where receipts are always required regardless of amount

- **All meals with clients or candidates** — itemized receipt required even if under $25, plus names of attendees and business purpose
- **All travel-related expenses** — receipts required regardless of amount (airfare, hotel, rental car)
- **All Anniversary Benefit travel stipend expenses** — see Section 8 for simplified requirement

### Missing receipts

For lost receipts under $75, submit a Missing Receipt Affidavit in Concur with vendor, date, and business purpose. Missing receipts over $75 require manager approval and may not be reimbursed. **Habitual missing receipts will be flagged for review** — submit no more than 3 affidavits per quarter.

## 6. Submission process and deadlines

- **Submit within 30 days** of incurring the expense. Expenses submitted more than **60 days** after the date incurred require VP-level approval and may not be reimbursed.
- **End-of-year deadline:** All expenses incurred in a calendar year must be submitted by **January 15** of the following year for tax and accounting purposes. Late expenses past this date are at risk of non-reimbursement.
- **Reimbursement timing:** Approved expenses are reimbursed to your direct-deposit account within **10 business days** of final approval.

Submission flow in Concur:
1. Create a new expense report, name it descriptively (e.g., "NYC client meetings, March 4–6")
2. Add line items, attach receipts
3. Assign each line to a project code (your manager can confirm correct codes)
4. Submit for manager approval
5. Manager approves → Finance reviews → reimbursement issued

## 7. Corporate cards

Eligible employees (typically Director-level and above, plus production roles with frequent travel) may be issued a **Keystone corporate card** (Amex). Corporate card transactions still require submission through Concur with the same receipt rules. Personal charges on the corporate card are not permitted and must be repaid within 30 days if accidentally incurred.

## 8. Anniversary Benefit travel stipend ($2,500)

The Keystone Anniversary Benefit travel stipend (see Benefits Overview, Section 6) is processed through Concur with a **simplified receipt requirement**:

- Submit travel-related expenses (airfare, lodging, ground transportation, meals during travel) under expense category `ANNIVERSARY-TRAVEL`
- Receipt requirement is reduced to: receipts required for any single expense over $75 (rather than the standard $25)
- The stipend caps at $2,500 across all submissions within the 12-month window — overage is not reimbursed
- The stipend is **taxable income** and will be reflected on your W-2
- The stipend **cannot be reimbursed as cash** — it must be tied to actual travel-related expenses

## 9. Mileage

Personal vehicle use for business is reimbursed at the **current IRS standard mileage rate** (updated annually each January in Concur). Mileage submissions should include:

- Date of travel
- Starting and ending location
- Business purpose
- Total miles

Commuting between your home and your regular work location is **not** reimbursable. Travel from home to a non-routine location (a client site, an off-lot meeting) is reimbursable for the portion exceeding your normal commute.

## 10. Production expenses

Employees working on a production may incur expenses governed by the production's budget and UPM. While the **process** (Concur, receipts, deadlines) remains the same, the **categories and approval thresholds** may differ on a per-production basis. Your production's UPM or production accountant will brief you on production-specific rules at the start of the project. When this policy and production-specific rules conflict, production rules govern for production-coded expenses.

## 11. Frequently confused points

- **The receipt threshold is $25, not $50, not $75.** Expenses of $25 or more require an itemized receipt.
- **"No receipt under $25" does not mean "no documentation under $25."** You still need date, vendor, amount, and business purpose for every expense.
- **Client/candidate meals always require a receipt**, even for a $12 coffee.
- **The Anniversary travel stipend uses a $75 receipt threshold**, not the standard $25 — it's an exception, not the rule.
- **The Anniversary stipend cannot be taken as cash.** It must be tied to actual travel expenses, submitted within 12 months.
- **Submit within 30 days.** Expenses older than 60 days require VP approval.
- **Corporate card personal charges must be repaid within 30 days** — they are not "free spending."
