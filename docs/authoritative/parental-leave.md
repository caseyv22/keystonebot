# Parental Leave Policy

**Document:** Parental Leave Policy
**Version:** 2.4
**Last updated:** September 18, 2025
**Owner:** People Operations — Benefits Team
**Applies to:** All full-time salaried employees, globally (some details vary by country — see Section 10)
**Contact:** hr@keystone.studio | leaves@keystone.studio

---

## 1. Overview

Keystone Studios offers fully paid parental leave to support employees becoming parents through birth, adoption, surrogacy, or foster placement. This policy applies to **all new parents regardless of gender, role, or family structure**.

There are two components to parental leave at Keystone:

- **Parental Bonding Leave** — 16 weeks of fully paid leave for all new parents
- **Medical Recovery Leave** — an additional 6 weeks of fully paid leave for the birthing parent

Together, the birthing parent at Keystone receives up to **22 weeks of fully paid leave**. Non-birthing parents receive **16 weeks of fully paid leave**. There is no difference in Parental Bonding Leave based on gender, biological relationship, or whether you are the primary or secondary caregiver.

## 2. Eligibility

You are eligible for parental leave if:

- You are a **full-time salaried employee**
- You have been employed at Keystone for at least **90 days** at the start of leave
- You are becoming a parent through **birth, adoption, surrogacy, or foster-to-adopt placement**

Project-based production crew on long-term contracts may be eligible — eligibility for non-salaried roles is reviewed case-by-case by People Operations.

## 3. Parental Bonding Leave (16 weeks)

All eligible new parents receive **16 weeks of fully paid Parental Bonding Leave** at 100% of base salary. Bonding Leave applies to:

- Birth of a child (either parent)
- Adoption of a child (either parent)
- Birth via surrogacy (either intended parent)
- Foster placement that is intended to lead to adoption

Bonding Leave must be taken **within 12 months** of the child's arrival (birth date, adoption finalization, or placement date). Bonding Leave can be taken in **up to two blocks** — for example, 12 weeks at arrival and 4 weeks later in the first year. Each block must be at least 2 weeks long. Splitting leave requires manager and People Operations approval at least 30 days before the second block begins.

## 4. Medical Recovery Leave (6 additional weeks for the birthing parent)

The **birthing parent** receives an additional **6 weeks of fully paid Medical Recovery Leave** to recover from childbirth. Medical Recovery Leave:

- Is **separate from and in addition to** the 16 weeks of Parental Bonding Leave
- Must be taken **immediately following the birth**, before any Parental Bonding Leave begins
- Can be extended by up to **2 additional weeks** with documentation from a healthcare provider in cases of cesarean delivery, complications, or extended recovery

A birthing parent who experiences pregnancy loss after 20 weeks of gestation is eligible for Medical Recovery Leave at the same terms. Pregnancy loss before 20 weeks is covered under Keystone's short-term disability and bereavement policies — contact leaves@keystone.studio for support.

## 5. Adoption, surrogacy, and foster placement

Parents becoming a family through adoption, surrogacy, or foster placement receive the full **16 weeks of Parental Bonding Leave**, with the following clarifications:

- **Adoption:** Leave begins on the date of legal placement or finalization, at the parent's choice
- **Surrogacy:** Either intended parent receives the full 16 weeks; the birthing surrogate is not a Keystone employee benefit recipient under this policy
- **Foster placement:** Leave applies when the placement is intended to lead to adoption. Short-term or respite foster placements are covered under PTO, not Parental Leave.

Keystone's **family-forming benefit** (see Benefits Overview, Section 8) covers up to $30,000 lifetime in adoption, fertility, and surrogacy costs and is separate from this leave policy.

## 6. Special situations

### Multiples (twins, triplets, etc.)

Parents of multiples receive the same 16 weeks of Bonding Leave per arrival event — leave is not multiplied per child. Birthing parents of multiples may qualify for extended Medical Recovery Leave with healthcare provider documentation.

### NICU stays

If your newborn requires a NICU stay, you may **pause** your Parental Bonding Leave clock during the NICU period and resume it when your child comes home. Contact leaves@keystone.studio to arrange this — it must be requested and documented at the time of the NICU admission, not retroactively. Medical Recovery Leave continues during the NICU stay and is not paused.

### Loss

In the event of stillbirth, infant loss, or pregnancy loss after 20 weeks, the birthing parent receives the full Medical Recovery Leave. Both parents are also eligible for **up to 4 weeks of bereavement leave** at 100% pay, taken at the family's pace, in coordination with People Operations.

### Loss of a child during foster-to-adopt placement

If a foster-to-adopt placement ends before adoption is finalized, the parent receives up to **2 weeks of paid bereavement leave** and may use any unused Bonding Leave within the original 12-month window if a new placement occurs.

## 7. How leave interacts with other benefits and policies

- **PTO:** Parental leave does **not** draw down your PTO balance. PTO continues to accrue during paid leave.
- **Lot Days:** If your leave overlaps the December lot shutdown, Lot Days that fall within paid leave are not used. They remain available for the year only if any of the shutdown window falls outside your leave period.
- **Benefits:** Medical, dental, vision, life, and disability coverage continue at the same employee contribution rate during paid parental leave.
- **401(k) and KVUs:** 401(k) contributions and company match continue based on your leave pay. KVU vesting continues uninterrupted during paid parental leave.
- **Holidays:** Company holidays that fall during paid parental leave do not extend your leave — they are absorbed into the leave period.

## 8. Requesting parental leave

Submit a Parental Leave Request through Workday or by emailing **leaves@keystone.studio**. We ask for at least **60 days' notice** when possible, recognizing that not all arrivals are predictable.

Your request should include:

- Expected arrival date (or placement date)
- Planned leave structure (one block or two)
- Planned start date and length of each block
- Contact info during leave (optional)

A member of the Leaves team will confirm your eligibility, schedule, and benefits coordination within **5 business days**, and will follow up with a written confirmation of your leave plan.

## 9. Return to work

You will be returned to your **same role and same base compensation** at the end of parental leave. If your role has materially changed during your leave (e.g., reorganization), People Operations and your manager will work with you on placement at an equivalent role and level.

Keystone supports gradual returns. With manager approval, you may return on a reduced schedule (e.g., 3 days/week) for up to **4 weeks** following the end of leave, with full pay continuing during that ramp.

A **lactation support program** is available for returning birthing parents. Lactation rooms are available on the Burbank lot in Buildings One, Three, and Five, and equivalent accommodations are provided at satellite offices. For travel during the first 12 months postpartum, Keystone covers shipping costs for expressed milk — submit through Expenses with the code `LACTATION-SHIP`.

## 10. International employees

Employees in Vancouver, London, and other international offices receive parental leave benefits that meet or exceed local statutory requirements. In most cases, Keystone's 16+6 structure is more generous than statutory minimums, in which case Keystone's terms apply. Where local law provides more generous terms, those terms apply. Contact leaves@keystone.studio for your country-specific details.

## 11. Frequently confused points

- **Parental Bonding Leave is 16 weeks, not 12, not 8.** This applies to all new parents regardless of gender or role.
- **Medical Recovery Leave is only for the birthing parent**, and it is **on top of** Bonding Leave, not part of it.
- **Adoptive and surrogacy parents receive the full 16 weeks** of Bonding Leave — leave is not reduced because there is no birth event.
- **Parental leave does not draw down PTO.** PTO continues to accrue during leave.
- **Leave can be split into two blocks**, but each must be at least 2 weeks and split must be approved in advance.
- **The 12-month window is from the child's arrival**, not from when you tell HR.

## 12. Questions

For parental leave questions, contact **leaves@keystone.studio**. For general People Operations questions, **hr@keystone.studio** or the `#ask-people-ops` Slack channel. The Leaves team will assign you a dedicated point of contact once your request is submitted.
