# Code of Conduct

**Document:** Code of Conduct
**Version:** 6.1
**Last updated:** November 4, 2025
**Owner:** Legal & Compliance, in partnership with People Operations
**Applies to:** All employees, contractors, freelancers, interns, and production crew working under Keystone Studios — globally
**Contact:** ethics@keystone.studio | hr@keystone.studio | EthicsPoint hotline: see Section 11

---

## 1. Overview

This Code of Conduct sets the expectations for how we work at Keystone Studios. It applies to everyone working under the Keystone name — whether you're salaried corporate staff in Burbank, a freelance editor in post, a unit production assistant on a shoot in Atlanta, or a ride operator at KeystoneLand.

The Code is built on five commitments:

1. **We treat people with respect.** No harassment, no discrimination, no retaliation.
2. **We act with integrity.** No conflicts of interest, no fraud, no shortcuts.
3. **We protect what's confidential.** Unreleased projects, employee data, business strategy.
4. **We follow the law.** Every jurisdiction we work in, without exception.
5. **We speak up when something is wrong.** And we protect the people who do.

If you ever face a situation where doing your job seems to conflict with this Code, the Code wins. Stop, ask, escalate. That is always the right move.

## 2. Respect in the workplace

### Harassment and discrimination

Keystone has **zero tolerance** for harassment or discrimination of any kind, including (but not limited to) on the basis of: race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, age, disability, pregnancy, marital or family status, military or veteran status, or any other protected characteristic.

Harassment includes:
- Unwelcome physical contact
- Verbal abuse, slurs, or threats
- Visual harassment (images, written content)
- Sexual harassment, including unwelcome advances, requests for sexual favors, or creating a sexually hostile environment
- Bullying, intimidation, or repeated belittling behavior

This applies on the lot, on location, at company events, in Slack and email, in video meetings, on social media when interacting with coworkers, and at industry events where you are representing Keystone.

### Romantic and sexual relationships at work

Consensual relationships between coworkers are not prohibited, but **romantic or sexual relationships across a direct reporting line are not permitted.** If a relationship develops with someone in your reporting chain (in either direction), one of you must disclose to People Operations within **30 days** so a reporting reassignment can be arranged. Non-disclosure is a violation of this Code.

Relationships involving production hierarchy (e.g., showrunner/cast, director/crew on a production you are directly supervising) are subject to the same rule.

### Inclusivity

We work with people from many backgrounds and cultures. Make space for different perspectives. Use the names and pronouns people ask you to use. Ask questions when you don't know; don't make assumptions.

## 3. Integrity and conflicts of interest

### Conflicts of interest

A conflict of interest is any situation where your personal interests could compete with your responsibility to Keystone. You must **disclose potential conflicts in writing** to ethics@keystone.studio and your manager.

Common examples that require disclosure:
- Significant personal financial interest in a Keystone vendor, partner, or competitor
- A close family member working at a Keystone vendor, partner, or competitor in a role that touches your work
- Outside employment, consulting, or board roles in the media/entertainment industry
- Personal relationships (romantic, family, close friendship) with someone whose work decisions you influence
- Side projects or production work outside Keystone that could overlap with Keystone IP or business

Outside employment is generally permitted as long as it does not compete with Keystone, does not use Keystone resources, does not occur during working hours, and is disclosed.

### Gifts and entertainment

You may **accept** modest, business-appropriate gifts (e.g., a vendor sends a holiday gift basket worth under **$100**). Anything over $100 must be declined, returned, or disclosed to ethics@keystone.studio for review. Cash and cash equivalents (gift cards, prepaid cards) of any amount must be declined regardless of value.

You may **give** modest, business-appropriate gifts to clients, partners, and vendors when reasonable. Gifts over **$250** per recipient per year require manager approval. Gifts to government officials require Legal & Compliance pre-approval **without exception** — this includes meals, swag, premiere tickets, KeystoneLand passes, or anything of value.

### Fraud, theft, and misuse of company resources

Falsifying expense reports, time tracking, performance data, or any business record is grounds for termination and may result in legal action. The same applies to theft of Keystone property, misuse of corporate cards, and unauthorized personal use of Keystone resources at material scale.

Modest, reasonable personal use of Keystone resources (printing a personal document, taking a personal phone call from your desk, occasional personal email on your work laptop) is fine. Use judgment.

## 4. Confidentiality and information security

### Confidential information

You are entrusted with information that is not public, including:

- **Unreleased projects** — scripts, dailies, cuts, marketing plans, release dates, casting decisions
- **Business strategy** — financials, deals in negotiation, M&A activity, internal org changes
- **Employee data** — compensation, performance, personal info, medical and leave information
- **Vendor and partner information** — contracts, pricing, deal terms

Do not discuss any of the above outside of the people who need to know it for their work, including with family members, friends, on social media, in public spaces where you can be overheard, or with the press.

### Press and external communications

If a journalist, blogger, podcaster, or social-media account contacts you about Keystone, **do not respond**. Forward the inquiry to **comms@keystone.studio**. This applies even if the question seems harmless ("can you confirm you work there?") or the person seems friendly.

You may, of course, list Keystone as your employer on personal social media. What you cannot do is speak for Keystone, leak project details, or amplify unverified rumors about the company.

### Information security

- Use strong, unique passwords and Keystone's SSO and MFA
- Lock your screen when stepping away
- Don't connect personal devices to production-rated networks without IT approval
- Don't share screenshots of unreleased work in public Slack channels, social media, or messaging apps
- Report suspected phishing or security incidents immediately to **security@keystone.studio** and the `#sec-incidents` Slack channel

### Social media

You can have a personal social media presence. When posting:
- Don't share unreleased Keystone content (including photos from set, screening rooms, edit bays, or anywhere on the lot showing in-progress work)
- Don't speak for Keystone unless your role specifically authorizes it
- Don't engage in harassment of coworkers, clients, talent, or partners — public-facing harassment is treated the same as workplace harassment
- Don't share confidential information of any kind

Posts that violate this Code can result in disciplinary action regardless of whether your account is "personal."

## 5. On the lot and on set

Keystone's physical environments have additional behavioral expectations:

- **Talent and guest interactions:** Do not approach, photograph, or solicit autographs from talent, executives, or guests on the lot or on set unless your job requires it. This applies to your personal time on the lot as well.
- **Set safety:** Follow all instructions from the 1st AD, safety officer, and stunt coordinator. Safety is non-negotiable.
- **Substances:** Drugs and alcohol are prohibited on the lot and on set during working hours, except at sanctioned company events where alcohol is being served. Cannabis use is prohibited on the lot regardless of state legality.
- **Weapons:** No personal weapons on the lot or on set. Prop weapons are governed by the prop master and 1st AD.

## 6. KeystoneLand-specific expectations

Employees working at KeystoneLand (Las Vegas) operate in a guest-facing environment with additional standards:

- Always be in uniform / costume during shifts, per role
- Maintain "in-character" professionalism in guest-facing areas
- Guest safety, especially child safety, is the highest priority
- Do not photograph guests; do not share photos of guests on personal social media
- Lost children protocols and emergency procedures are covered in KeystoneLand operations training and override anything in this Code in conflict

## 7. Outside activities

Outside employment, consulting, and creative side projects are generally permitted with disclosure. The exceptions:

- **Direct competitors** — you may not work for a Keystone competitor while employed at Keystone
- **Use of Keystone IP** — you may not develop personal projects using Keystone characters, scripts, footage, music, or branded assets
- **Use of Keystone time and resources** — outside work cannot occur during your Keystone working hours or use Keystone equipment
- **Use of your Keystone affiliation** — you may not represent your personal projects as endorsed by or affiliated with Keystone

Disclose outside activities to **ethics@keystone.studio** when you begin them and update annually during the Conflicts Disclosure cycle each fall.

## 8. Government, lobbying, and political activity

- Personal political activity is your own business and is protected
- You may **not** use Keystone resources, time, or your Keystone title for personal political activity
- Lobbying activity on Keystone's behalf is coordinated solely by the Government Affairs team
- Political contributions on behalf of Keystone require pre-approval from Legal & Compliance and Government Affairs

## 9. Anti-bribery and anti-corruption

Keystone follows the **US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act** and equivalent anti-bribery laws in every jurisdiction we work in. Bribery — offering anything of value to influence a business or government decision — is prohibited without exception. This applies even in jurisdictions where local practice may suggest otherwise.

If you are working internationally and someone requests a payment, gift, or favor in connection with a business or permitting decision, **stop and contact Legal & Compliance immediately** at **legal@keystone.studio**.

## 10. Production-specific considerations

Productions operate under additional rules driven by guild agreements, union rules, child labor law (for minor performers), location-specific permitting, and intimacy coordination protocols. These are not exhaustively documented here — your production's UPM, intimacy coordinator, and on-set HR partner will brief you. When production-specific rules conflict with this Code, **the stricter rule applies**.

## 11. Reporting concerns

If you experience or witness conduct that may violate this Code, report it. Keystone offers multiple channels:

- **Your manager** — usually the fastest path for non-sensitive concerns
- **Skip-level manager** — when your direct manager is unavailable or involved
- **People Operations** — hr@keystone.studio or `#ask-people-ops` Slack
- **Legal & Compliance** — ethics@keystone.studio for ethics-related concerns
- **EthicsPoint hotline** — **1-844-KEYSTONE** (1-844-539-7866) or **keystonestudios.ethicspoint.com** — available 24/7, can be used **anonymously**, operated by a third-party
- **Ombuds Office** — for confidential, off-the-record conversations to help you think through a situation, **ombuds@keystone.studio**. The Ombuds is independent of management and does not file formal reports without your consent.

### Non-retaliation

Keystone **prohibits retaliation** against anyone who reports a concern in good faith, participates in an investigation, or refuses to engage in conduct they reasonably believe violates this Code. Retaliation is itself a violation of this Code and is treated as a separate, serious matter.

"In good faith" does not require you to be right — it requires that you had a reasonable basis to believe a violation occurred. Honest mistakes are protected. Knowingly false reports are not.

## 12. Investigations

When a concern is reported, Legal & Compliance or People Operations will:

- Acknowledge receipt within **3 business days**
- Conduct a fair, prompt, and confidential investigation appropriate to the nature of the concern
- Communicate the outcome to the reporter to the extent permitted by law and confidentiality

We cannot always share full investigation details with the reporter, but we will always confirm that the matter was reviewed.

## 13. Consequences for violations

Violations of this Code may result in:

- Coaching, training, or a written warning
- Reassignment or removal from a project
- Suspension
- Termination of employment
- Referral to law enforcement and/or civil legal action

Consequences are determined based on the severity of the violation, intent, prior conduct, and any aggravating or mitigating factors. Some violations — harassment, retaliation, fraud, bribery, theft, breach of safety protocol — may result in immediate termination on first offense.

## 14. Frequently confused points

- **Zero tolerance for harassment means investigated, not automatically terminated.** Every report is investigated; consequences are determined by what the investigation finds. "Zero tolerance" does not mean "guilty without process."
- **Romantic relationships at work are allowed**, but **not across a direct reporting line.** And they must be disclosed within 30 days.
- **Gifts over $100 must be disclosed**, not automatically returned. The Ethics team makes the call.
- **Cash and gift cards must be declined regardless of value.** No exception, even for nominal amounts.
- **Reporting can be anonymous** via EthicsPoint. You do not have to give your name.
- **Non-retaliation applies even if the report turns out to be wrong**, as long as it was made in good faith.
- **Social media posts about Keystone work** — including photos from the lot or set — can violate this Code even on "personal" accounts.

## 15. Questions

For Code interpretation, contact **ethics@keystone.studio** or your manager. For HR-specific concerns, **hr@keystone.studio**. For confidential consultations, **ombuds@keystone.studio**. To report a concern, use any channel listed in Section 11.
