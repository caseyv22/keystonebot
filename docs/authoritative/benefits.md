## 1. Overview

Keystone Studios offers a benefits package designed for a workforce that mixes salaried corporate staff with project-based production crew. This document is your top-level guide. For plan-specific details (provider networks, formularies, fund options), see the linked plan documents on the People Operations Confluence space.

Your benefits at Keystone include:

- Medical, dental, and vision coverage
- Health savings and flexible spending accounts
- 401(k) retirement plan with company match
- Keystone Value Units (KVUs) — Keystone's phantom equity program
- The Keystone Anniversary Benefit
- Life and disability insurance
- Mental health and wellness resources
- Family-forming benefits

This document does **not** cover PTO, parental leave, perks, or expenses — those have their own policies.

## 2. Medical, dental, and vision

Coverage begins on your **first day of employment** — there is no waiting period. You have **30 days from your start date** to enroll. If you do not enroll within 30 days, you will be auto-enrolled in the default plans listed below and cannot change your election until the next open enrollment unless you experience a qualifying life event.

### Medical

Keystone offers three medical plans through our carrier:

- **Keystone Core PPO** — broad network, moderate premiums, standard deductible
- **Keystone Plus PPO** — broad network, higher premiums, lower deductible, lower out-of-pocket max
- **Keystone HDHP** — high-deductible plan paired with an HSA, lowest premiums

Keystone pays **90% of the employee-only premium** on the Core and HDHP plans, and **80% of the employee-only premium** on the Plus plan. For dependent coverage, Keystone pays **70% of the additional premium** across all three plans.

### Dental

One PPO dental plan. Keystone covers **100% of preventive care** (cleanings, exams, x-rays twice per year), and the plan includes orthodontia coverage for both children and adults.

### Vision

One vision plan covering an annual eye exam, frames or contacts allowance every 12 months, and discounts on additional purchases.

## 3. HSA and FSA

If you enroll in the HDHP, you are eligible for a **Health Savings Account (HSA)**. Keystone contributes:

- **$1,200/year** for employee-only HDHP coverage
- **$2,400/year** for family HDHP coverage

Contributions are deposited in quarterly installments. You can contribute additional pre-tax dollars up to the annual IRS limit.

If you enroll in the Core or Plus PPO plans, you are eligible for a **Healthcare FSA** and a **Dependent Care FSA**, both up to the annual IRS limits. FSA funds follow the IRS use-it-or-lose-it rules with a $640 carryover allowed on the Healthcare FSA. Dependent Care FSA balances do not carry over.

## 4. 401(k) retirement plan

You are eligible for the Keystone 401(k) plan on your **first day of employment**.

- **Company match:** Keystone matches **100% of the first 5%** of your eligible compensation that you contribute. The match is contributed each pay period alongside your own contribution.
- **Vesting:** The company match vests on a **3-year cliff schedule** — you become 100% vested in the company match after completing 3 years of service. Your own contributions are always 100% vested.
- **Auto-enrollment:** New employees are auto-enrolled at a **5% pre-tax contribution rate** after 30 days unless they opt out or change their election in Workday.
- **Roth option:** Available alongside traditional pre-tax contributions.

If you leave Keystone before completing 3 years of service, **you forfeit the unvested company match**, but you keep 100% of your own contributions and their earnings.

## 5. Keystone Value Units (KVUs)

Keystone is privately held, so we do not offer publicly traded stock. Instead, eligible employees receive **Keystone Value Units (KVUs)** — a phantom equity program designed to function similarly to RSUs at a public company.

KVU basics:

- Granted at hire (new hire grant) and annually thereafter (refresh grant) based on role, level, and performance
- Vest over **4 years on a 1-year cliff, then quarterly** thereafter
- Pay out in **cash** based on the company's most recent annual valuation
- Vested but unpaid KVUs settle annually each March based on the prior fiscal year's valuation

KVU grant amounts are confidential and communicated individually in your offer letter and in your annual compensation review. For program mechanics and tax treatment, see the KVU Plan Document on Confluence.

## 6. The Keystone Anniversary Benefit

At every five-year service anniversary (5, 10, 15, 20, 25 years), you receive:

- **A two-week paid sabbatical** — in addition to PTO, must be taken within 12 months of the anniversary date, can be taken in one block or split into two one-week blocks with manager approval
- **A $2,500 travel stipend** — must be used within 12 months of the anniversary date, processed through Expenses with a simplified receipt requirement (see Expense Reimbursement Policy)

**Important rules:**

- The travel stipend is **taxable income** and will be reflected on your W-2
- The travel stipend **cannot be taken as cash** — it must be used toward travel-related expenses (airfare, lodging, ground transportation, meals during travel) and submitted through Expenses
- The sabbatical **does not draw down your PTO balance** and does not affect KVU vesting
- If you leave Keystone before using the sabbatical or stipend within the 12-month window, **both are forfeited**

## 7. Life and disability insurance

Keystone provides at no cost to you:

- **Basic life insurance** — 2x your annual base salary, up to $500,000
- **Short-term disability** — covers 60% of base salary for up to 26 weeks
- **Long-term disability** — covers 60% of base salary after STD ends, up to age 65

You may purchase supplemental life and AD&D coverage during enrollment.

## 8. Mental health, wellness, and family forming

- **Employee Assistance Program (EAP)** — **8 free counseling sessions per issue per year**, available 24/7. Confidential and separate from your medical plan.
- **Talkspace and Headspace subscriptions** — provided at no cost to all employees
- **Family-forming benefits** — up to **$30,000 lifetime maximum** toward fertility treatment, adoption, or surrogacy costs through our family-forming partner
- **Bereavement leave** — see PTO Policy Section 6

For monthly wellness reimbursements (gym, fitness apps), see the Perks & Programs document — that benefit lives there, not here.

## 9. International coverage

Benefits vary by country. The structures above describe the US plan. Employees in Vancouver, London, and other international offices receive equivalent local benefits packages designed to meet or exceed local market standards. For details on your country-specific plan, contact benefits@keystone.studio.

## 10. Open enrollment and qualifying life events

- **Open enrollment** runs each November for the following calendar year. You will be notified by email at least 30 days before the enrollment window opens.
- **Qualifying life events** (marriage, divorce, birth or adoption of a child, loss of other coverage, etc.) allow mid-year changes. You have **30 days from the event** to make changes in Workday.

## 11. Frequently confused points

- **The 401(k) match is 100% of the first 5%**, not 50% of 6% or any other combination. The match is contributed each pay period, not annually.
- **The 3-year vesting cliff** applies only to the company match, not to your own contributions. Your own contributions are always 100% yours.
- **KVUs pay out in cash, not stock.** Keystone is private; there are no shares to issue.
- **The Anniversary Benefit travel stipend cannot be taken as cash.** It is a use-it-or-lose-it travel benefit, processed through Expenses.
- **HSA contributions from Keystone are quarterly**, not lump-sum at the start of the year.
- **There is no waiting period** for medical, dental, vision, or 401(k) eligibility. All are effective on your first day.
