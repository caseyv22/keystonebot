## 2. Founders' Fridays

### What it is

**The last Friday of every month is a company-wide no-meetings day.** Originally a founder-era tradition ("we built this place on Fridays without meetings"), now formalized in the handbook.

### The rules

- **No internal meetings** are scheduled on the last Friday of each month, company-wide
- Slack notifications are **encouraged to be paused** during the day — not required
- It is **not a day off** — you are expected to work, just without meetings
- **External meetings** (clients, partners, candidates) are permitted but discouraged
- Use the day for focus work, deep thinking, learning, or whatever moves your work forward without meeting interruption

### Exceptions

- **Production teams in active shoot** are exempt — productions cannot pause for a calendar day
- **Emergency response, security, and on-call rotations** continue normally
- **Critical client/partner moments** can override Founders' Fridays with VP-level approval

### How to access

Automatic. Calendars are blocked for all employees on Founders' Fridays — you cannot accept a new internal meeting on that day without overriding the block.

## 3. Learning & Development (L&D) stipend

### What it is

An annual stipend for professional development — courses, conferences, books, certifications, software for learning.

### The amount and eligibility

- **$2,000 per calendar year**, available to all full-time salaried employees
- Eligibility begins on your **first day of employment**
- The stipend resets each January 1 — **it does not roll over**

### What's reimbursable

- Online courses (Coursera, MasterClass, Udemy, LinkedIn Learning, etc.)
- Conference registration and conference travel (separate from the conference fee, you may submit travel under your team budget if applicable)
- Books, audiobooks, and digital subscriptions related to your professional development
- Certifications and exam fees
- Coaching (executive coaching, communication coaching, etc.) with manager approval
- Software and tools used for learning (e.g., a Figma license for someone learning design)

### What's not reimbursable

- Degree programs requiring multi-year commitment (these go through a separate Tuition Assistance program — contact perks@keystone.studio)
- Tools you already use for your current job (those are submitted as standard business expenses)
- Travel and accommodations unrelated to learning (those go through Expense Reimbursement)

### How to access

Submit through Concur using the category `LND-STIPEND`. Manager approval is required for items over $500. No approval needed under $500. The full $2,000 must be submitted by **December 15** of the calendar year — late submissions cannot be honored due to year-end accounting.

## 4. Wellness reimbursement

### What it is

A monthly reimbursement for fitness, mental health, and wellness expenses.

### The amount and eligibility

- **$75 per month**, available to all full-time salaried employees
- Eligibility begins on your **first day of employment**
- Submissions are **monthly**, with a rolling **90-day window** to claim each month — for example, your January wellness expenses must be submitted by April 1

### What's reimbursable

- Gym memberships
- Fitness studio classes (yoga, pilates, Crossfit, climbing, etc.)
- Fitness apps and subscriptions (Strava Premium, Peloton app, Apple Fitness+, etc.)
- Home fitness equipment, prorated up to $75/month (a $900 stationary bike could be reimbursed as 12 months of wellness)
- Massage and bodywork at licensed providers
- Mental health apps not covered by EAP/Talkspace/Headspace (which are provided free — see Benefits)
- Wellness retreats (registration only, not travel)

### What's not reimbursable

- Athletic apparel and shoes
- Supplements, vitamins, and meal-delivery services
- Cosmetic procedures
- Items already covered by your medical plan

### How to access

Submit through Concur using category `WELLNESS`. No manager approval required under $75/month. Itemized receipts required regardless of amount (this is an exception to the standard $25 receipt rule, because wellness reimbursement requires verification of what was purchased).

## 5. Home office stipend

### What it is

Funding for employees to set up and maintain a home workspace.

### The amount and eligibility

- **New hire setup: $1,500 one-time**, submitted within the first **90 days** of employment
- **Annual refresh: $300/year**, available each January starting in the calendar year after your hire date
- Available to **all full-time salaried employees**, including those who work primarily from the lot — Keystone recognizes that hybrid work is the norm

### What's reimbursable

- Monitor(s), keyboard, mouse, webcam, headphones, microphone
- Desk, desk chair, desk lamp, standing desk converters
- Home internet upgrade (one-time setup costs only, not monthly fees)
- Cables, hubs, and accessories

### What's not reimbursable

- General home furniture not used as a workspace
- Personal electronics (TVs, phones for personal use, gaming equipment)
- Internet monthly bills (covered separately by some teams via the Tech team allowance — ask your manager)
- Decor not related to work setup

### How to access

Submit through Concur using category `HOME-OFFICE`. Manager approval required for any single item over $500. Items purchased with the stipend remain your personal property — you do not return them when you leave Keystone.

## 6. Pet policy on the lot

### What it is

Dogs are welcome on the Burbank lot in designated buildings on designated days, under the Keystone Pet Program.

### Eligibility and rules

- **Dogs only.** No other pets, including cats, birds, reptiles, or service animals other than ADA-recognized service animals (which are always permitted in all areas under federal law).
- **Designated buildings:** Building One, Building Three, and the Quad outdoor area. Dogs are **not permitted** in Buildings Two (post-production / quiet work), Four (soundstages), or Five (executive offices and screening room).
- **Designated days:** Tuesdays and Thursdays only. No dogs on the lot on other weekdays or weekends, regardless of building.
- **Registration required.** Register your dog through the Pet Program portal (linked in Workday) before bringing them on the lot. Registration requires proof of current vaccinations, liability waiver, and one-time $25 admin fee.
- **Behavior expectations:** Dogs must be leashed or in a carrier at all times. Aggressive behavior, excessive barking, or accidents indoors result in revoked privileges.
- **Allergy and fear accommodations:** If a coworker requests a pet-free zone for medical or comfort reasons, Pet Program staff will mediate and rearrange seating if needed.

### How to access

Register at the Pet Program portal in Workday. You'll receive a Keystone-branded ID tag for your dog's collar within 5 business days.

## 7. Employee screenings and premieres

### What it is

Access to Keystone film and TV premieres, advance screenings on the lot, and select industry events.

### What's included

- **On-lot advance screenings:** Held in the Building Five screening room. Open to all employees, RSVP required. Typically held 1–2 weeks before a project's public release.
- **Premiere tickets:** Allocated for Keystone-produced theatrical releases. Allocation varies by project — some premieres have broad employee distribution, others are limited to credited cast/crew plus a small employee lottery.
- **Industry event passes:** Occasional access to film festivals, awards shows, and industry trade events where Keystone has presence — these are highly limited and distributed by lottery.

### The rules

- **Plus-one policy:** For most events, you may bring one guest. Premiere tickets to specific events may be employee-only depending on the venue.
- **Tickets are not transferable.** You must attend with your guest; you cannot give your tickets to a friend who works elsewhere.
- **Lottery-based events:** When demand exceeds supply, allocation is by random employee lottery. You can enter through Workday; results are posted 2 weeks before the event.
- **Resale is prohibited.** Selling or transferring tickets is a violation of this perk and may result in losing future access.

### How to access

Watch the `#screenings-and-premieres` Slack channel for event announcements. RSVP and lottery entry are managed through Workday.

## 8. KeystoneLand employee tickets

### What it is

Complimentary admission to KeystoneLand (Las Vegas, NV) for Keystone employees and their accompanying guests.

### The allocation

- **2 complimentary tickets per quarter**, totaling **8 tickets per calendar year**
- Tickets are issued automatically at the start of each quarter (Jan 1, Apr 1, Jul 1, Oct 1)
- Available to **all full-time salaried employees** — corporate staff and KeystoneLand operations staff alike

### Rollover rules

- Unused quarterly tickets **roll over within the same calendar year only**
- **All unused tickets expire on December 31** of the year they were issued — they do not carry into the next calendar year
- You can therefore accumulate up to 8 tickets at most by Q4 if you haven't used any earlier in the year, but starting Jan 1 your balance resets to your new Q1 allocation

### Transferability

- **Tickets are not transferable.** You, the employee, must be present at the gate with your valid Keystone ID to redeem any ticket — yours or your guests'.
- You may bring **guests of your choice** with you (family, friends, partners), and they enter with you using your ticket allocation. The tickets are not "yours plus your spouse only" — they're "you plus up to N guests you've chosen, with you present."
- You **cannot give a ticket to a non-employee to use without you.** Tickets handed off without the employee present will be invalidated at the gate.

### Blackout dates

- **There are no blackout dates.** Your tickets are valid any day KeystoneLand is open.

### In-park discounts

- All full-time employees receive a **20% discount on food and merchandise** purchased inside KeystoneLand, on any day, with valid Keystone ID at point of purchase.
- The discount applies whether or not you are using a complimentary ticket that day — you can visit the park on a personal trip and still get the discount.

### Annual pass

- **Keystone does not offer an employee annual pass program.** The 2-tickets-per-quarter allocation is the entire employee ticket benefit.

### How to access

Tickets are issued digitally through the KeystoneLand mobile app linked to your Keystone employee account. At the start of each quarter, you'll receive an email confirming your new ticket allocation. Present the digital ticket and your Keystone ID at any park entrance.

## 9. Perks at separation

If you leave Keystone (voluntarily or involuntarily):

- **Unused L&D stipend, wellness reimbursement, and home office stipend:** Not paid out or carried over
- **Unused KeystoneLand tickets:** Forfeited as of your last day
- **Home office equipment purchased with the stipend:** Remains your personal property
- **20% park discount and other employee perks:** End on your last day

## 10. Frequently confused points

- **Founders' Fridays are not days off.** No internal meetings, but you are expected to work.
- **The L&D stipend does not roll over.** $2,000/year, resets each January.
- **The wellness reimbursement is $75 per month**, not $75 annually or $900 lump sum. It can only be claimed monthly.
- **The home office stipend has two tiers:** $1,500 one-time at hire, $300/year ongoing.
- **Dogs are allowed on the lot only on Tuesdays and Thursdays**, only in specific buildings — not every day, not everywhere.
- **KeystoneLand tickets do roll over within the calendar year**, but they **do not carry across years.** All unused tickets expire December 31.
- **KeystoneLand tickets are not transferable.** You must be at the gate; you cannot hand them to a friend.
- **There is no Keystone employee annual pass for KeystoneLand.** The 2-per-quarter allocation is the entire benefit.
- **There are no blackout dates** on KeystoneLand employee tickets.
- **Premiere tickets are not transferable**, and resale is prohibited.
