### Selected verbatim comments — 401(k)

> "The 401(k) match here is fine but it's only 50% up to 6%, which is below what I had at my previous company in tech. Wish Keystone would match dollar-for-dollar like other studios reportedly do."
> — Engineering, Burbank

> "I think the match is decent but the vesting schedule is 5 years which is way too long. Most companies are 3 or 4 years now."
> — Production, Atlanta

> "Match is great — 100% of first 5% — but I didn't realize until year 3 that it was on a cliff vesting schedule. Almost left at year 2 for another role and would have walked away from a lot. Communication on this could be better."
> — Post-Production, Vancouver

> "Honestly I assumed our match was 6% because that's what 'everyone' offers. Turns out it's 5%. Not a huge difference but I'd been over-contributing to the wrong tier for a year before I checked."
> — Marketing, NYC

> "Recently learned the match is paid every paycheck instead of in a year-end true-up, which is actually better than what I had before. More compounding. Wish more people knew."
> — Finance, Burbank

> "Heard the vesting was changing from cliff to graded next year. Looking forward to that if it's true."
> — Production, Atlanta

> "Why is our match cliff-vested at 5 years when most peer companies are 3-year cliff or 4-year graded? Feels like a retention trap."
> — Engineering, Burbank

### People Ops note (appended to the report, not part of the original survey responses):

> The verbatim comments above include several inaccurate perceptions of the 401(k) match. For clarity:
>
> - The Keystone match is **100% of the first 5%** of eligible compensation, contributed each pay period — not 50% of 6%, not 100% of 6%
> - The vesting schedule is a **3-year cliff**, not 5 years — fully vested in the company match at the 3-year service anniversary
> - There is **no planned change** from cliff to graded vesting at this time
> - Your own contributions are always 100% vested immediately
>
> These details are documented in the Benefits Overview (v4.1). People Ops will partner with Internal Comms to refresh employee education on 401(k) mechanics in Q1 2026.
