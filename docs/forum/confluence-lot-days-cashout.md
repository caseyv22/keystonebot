## Year-end PTO and Lot Days — Atlanta perspective

A few tips and quirks I've learned over the years (5+ at Keystone Atlanta) for end-of-year planning:

### Lot Days

Since we're not on the Burbank lot, **Lot Days are a little weird for us.** Technically you're supposed to use them during the Burbank shutdown window, but in practice the Atlanta office stays open through most of late December because of post-production timelines. A few things I've figured out:

- **You can cash out unused Lot Days** if you can show you couldn't use them due to production needs. I've done this twice — it goes through your UPM and then production accounting handles it as a small bonus on your January paycheck. It's not advertised but it's an option.
- Alternatively, your UPM can sometimes get you the days back as **floating PTO** to use the following year if you missed the window
- The 5 Lot Days don't roll over normally, but the cash-out path effectively does the same thing

### PTO at year-end

PTO is more straightforward. Standard accrual, 240 cap (this one IS enforced, I've hit it). Just plan your December PTO requests by early November to beat the rush.
