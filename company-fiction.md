# Keystone Studios — Company Fiction

The source-of-truth one-pager for the fictional company KeystoneBot serves. All authoritative HR docs and forum posts should be consistent with the facts below. When in doubt, this file wins.

---

## Snapshot

- **Founded:** 2008, Burbank, CA
- **Headquarters:** Keystone Lot, Burbank — a 14-acre campus with three soundstages, a post-production building, and the original brick-front "Building One" where the company started
- **Satellite offices:** Atlanta (production), Vancouver (animation/VFX), New York (unscripted & ad sales), London (international development)
- **Theme park:** KeystoneLand, Las Vegas, NV — opened 2017 (see "KeystoneLand" section below)
- **Employees:** ~5,200 full-time globally — roughly 60% production/creative, 25% technology & post, 15% corporate (KeystoneLand operations staff counted separately under the park's own org)
- **Ownership:** Privately held. Founder-led through 2019, now run by a CEO brought in from a major streamer.

## What Keystone makes

Mid-budget prestige film, premium scripted TV, a growing unscripted slate, and a small but well-funded interactive division ("Keystone Interactive") that develops companion apps and a handful of original mobile games tied to Keystone IP. The company licenses to all the majors — no in-house streaming platform, which leadership treats as a strategic advantage ("we sell to everyone"). In 2017, Keystone opened **KeystoneLand**, a theme park in Las Vegas built around the company's film and TV IP — a deliberate bet on owning a physical fan destination without competing with the streamers.

## The vibe

Professional but not stuffy. Keystone hires people who could work at a tech company *or* a studio and want both — the creative energy of a production company, the comp structure and tooling of a tech company. Internally people call it "the lot that runs like a software company." Slack-first, OKRs, well-funded L&D, but also: physical lot, craft services, a screening room, and a culture that respects the people who actually make the shows.

## Comp & benefits posture

Above-market base, meaningful equity-equivalent units (Keystone is private, so it's a phantom equity / RSU-equivalent program called **Keystone Value Units**), strong health coverage, and benefits designed for a workforce that mixes salaried corporate staff with project-based production crew.

## Signature policies (the quirky-but-real details)

### 1. PTO: "25 + 5" structure, capped accrual

Full-time salaried employees receive **25 days of PTO per year** (200 hours), accrued biweekly. On top of that, every employee gets **5 "Lot Days"** — paid days off that must be used between mid-December and early January when the lot goes dark for the holidays. Lot Days do not roll over and cannot be cashed out. PTO itself caps at **240 accrued hours** (30 days) — once you hit the cap, accrual pauses until you take time off. **No unlimited PTO.**

### 2. Founders' Fridays

The last Friday of every month is a company-wide no-meetings day. Originally a founder-era tradition ("we built this place on Fridays without meetings"), now formalized in the handbook. It's not a day off — people work — but no internal meetings can be scheduled, and Slack notifications are encouraged to be paused. Production teams in active shoot are exempt.

### 3. The Keystone Anniversary Benefit

At every five-year anniversary (5, 10, 15, 20…), employees receive a **two-week paid sabbatical** *plus* a **$2,500 travel stipend** that must be used within 12 months and cannot be taken as cash. The sabbatical is in addition to PTO. The stipend is taxable and processed through expenses with a simplified receipt requirement.

### 4. Parental leave: tiered by role, not gender

Keystone offers **16 weeks of fully paid parental leave** for all new parents (birth, adoption, surrogacy, foster placement), with an additional **6 weeks of medical recovery leave** for the birthing parent. Leave must be taken within 12 months of the child's arrival and can be taken in up to two blocks.

### 5. KeystoneLand employee tickets

All full-time Keystone employees (corporate and KeystoneLand operations staff alike) receive **2 complimentary KeystoneLand tickets per quarter**. Unused tickets **roll over within the calendar year** but reset to zero on January 1 — they do not carry across years. Tickets are **not transferable**: the employee must be present at the gate with valid Keystone ID to use them, and accompanying guests must enter with the employee. There are **no blackout dates**. Employees also receive a **20% discount on food and merchandise** inside the park with employee ID. There is no separate annual pass program for employees.

## Voice & tone guide for HR docs

- Professional, clear, slightly warm but not casual
- Active voice, second person ("you")
- Specific numbers wherever possible — exact day counts, hour caps, dollar amounts
- No corporate jargon ("synergy," "leverage," "circle back")
- Light references to the lot, soundstages, and production rhythm are welcome and make Keystone feel real
- Every policy doc should include a version number and "last updated" date
- HR contact: **hr@keystone.studio** (use consistently across all docs)
